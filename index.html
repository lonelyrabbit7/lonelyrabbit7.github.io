For almost 25 years, the number of people living in extreme poverty — on less than $1.90 per person per day was steadily

declining. But the trend was interrupted in 2020, when poverty rose due to the disruption caused by the COVID-19

crisis combined with the effects of conflict and climate change — which had already been slowing poverty reduction.

In many regions, there still exists serious poverty in food.
